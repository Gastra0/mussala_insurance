## General principles

- Most services are provided as **expense reimbursement**: the insured person pays for the service,
  collects the documents, and submits them; the insurer reimburses costs within the package limit.
- **Reimbursement — up to 15 business days** from the date of submitting correctly completed documents.
- The insurer **does not require original invoices for amounts up to 500 €**, except when the submitted
  copies are incomplete or unclear.
- Documents can be submitted through the **UNIQA portal**; through the Mussala platform —
  **app.mussalains.com**; or by email to **health@mussalains.com**.
- Almost everywhere, documents require: **an outpatient sheet / referral (MOH form No. 119/98)** with
  the doctor's signature and stamp (UIN), diagnosis, history, and objective condition; **an invoice with
  fiscal receipt** in the name of the insured person with a detailed list of services and prices.

## 1. Prevention

Preventive check-ups are carried out **once a year** according to a pre-agreed schedule determined by
the employer. After the check-up, each employee receives the results individually in a **sealed envelope**
with recommendations for consultations and additional tests if needed.

**Preventive package contents:**
1. **Specialist examination** (internal medicine / cardiologist / general practitioner): history taking
   and physical examination; height and weight measurement with BMI calculation; blood pressure (RR)
   measurement; 12-lead ECG.
2. **Ophthalmologist examination:** visual acuity test; color vision test.
3. **Clinical laboratory tests:** complete blood count (CBC), glucose, total cholesterol; LDL, HDL;
   urinalysis — chemical test with 9 parameters; TSH, FT3, FT4; liver enzymes AST, ALT.
4. **Breast ultrasound** (for women).
5. **Abdominal ultrasound.**

---

## 2. Additional options

- **Prevention by choice:** each insured person may use **up to 150 €** for preventive examinations by
  specialists of their own choice.
- **Including family members** (under the same conditions). Family members: spouse or a person in actual
  cohabitation at the same permanent address; biological and/or adopted dependent children — **up to 18
  years**, or **up to 26 years** for full-time students. The age limit does not apply to children who
  cannot live independently due to physical or mental disabilities. **Maximum age for inclusion — up to
  75 years.**

## 3. Outpatient treatment — limit 2 600 € per year per person

Within the insurer's network and under expense reimbursement terms.

1. **Primary consultations** (when an illness appears or a chronic condition worsens): doctors-specialists
   of all fields, **except** dietitians, nutritionists, and specialists in plastic and aesthetic surgery;
   senior/qualified specialist (habilitated doctor).
2. **Follow-up consultations / second opinion:** the same specialists (the same exceptions); consultation
   with two or more habilitated doctors for the same condition.
3. **Emergency medical care** — at night, on weekends, and on public holidays.
4. **Home visits and monitoring** — by a nurse or by a specialist doctor.
5. **Clinical laboratory tests** (within the scope needed for diagnosis): hematology, biochemistry,
   microbiology, CRP; chemical urine analysis with liquid reagents (protein, bilirubin, urobilinogen),
   sediment; hormones, serology, trace elements, parasitology; tumor markers, immunology, virology,
   genetics; vitamins; skin allergy tests.
6. **Clinical instrumental examinations:** functional tests, ultrasound, X-ray, endoscopic, radioisotope,
   angiographic examinations. **Computed tomography (CT) and MRI — up to 2 examinations per insurance
   year.**
7. **Morphological tests** (as prescribed): cytology, histology.
8. **Procedures in outpatient treatment** (without consumables): intramuscular and intravenous injections;
   surgical wound dressings; suture removal; placement of an intravenous system/catheter; intra-articular
   injections; application and removal of immobilization dressings; outpatient surgical interventions,
   including laser procedures — **up to 512 €**.
9. **Preparation for planned hospitalization:** examinations and consultations, laboratory, functional,
   ultrasound, and X-ray tests.
10. **Cryotherapy and/or laser therapy** for benign skin formations.
11. **Electrocoagulation — 256 €.**
12. **Oncology.**
13. **Day hospital.**
14. **Medical expertise and certification:** preparation for submission to LKK to determine temporary
    incapacity for work; medical certificates for employment, for category B drivers, for marriage.
15. **Monitoring of incurably ill patients.**

**Notes:**
- Services of **partner laboratories** (blood, imaging, and other tests) **always** require prior
  confirmation by email. This does not apply to services costing **up to 25,57 €**
  (health@mussalains.com).
- Highly specialized examinations (**CT, NMR, MRI**) are performed only **after prior approval by the
  insurer**.

## 4. Inpatient treatment — limit 11 000 € per year per person

Within and outside the network. Hospitalization takes place under a **clinical pathway** with an NZOK
referral issued by the family doctor or treating doctor. Treatment **is not covered** for a person without
valid health insurance rights.

1. **Hospitalization** in clinics and all inpatient departments — **up to 30 days per year**, but not more
   than **15 days** in an intensive care unit. Departments: anesthesiology and intensive care, pediatrics,
   neurology, ENT, obstetrics and pregnancy pathology, toxicology, orthopedics and traumatology,
   infectious diseases, ophthalmology, urology, hematology and oncology, general surgery (abdominal,
   thoracic, maxillofacial), vascular surgery and angiology, neurosurgery, cardiac surgery, laparoscopic
   and endoscopic, minimally invasive surgery; internal medicine — cardiology and invasive cardiology,
   gastroenterology, neurology, endocrinology.
2. **Admission and accommodation — 310 €:** ward with 3+ beds; two-bed ward; single room / improved
   conditions.
3. **Examination and diagnostic/treatment plan:** by the treating doctor; by a doctor chosen by the
   patient; by the head of department.
4. **Rounds and monitoring:** treating doctor — daily; head of department — up to 2 times per week;
   licensed specialist — once per week.
5. **Consultations during hospitalization:** with a specialist of the facility; with a licensed specialist
   of the same facility; with a licensed specialist from another facility — one consultation per
   hospitalization.
6. **Clinical, laboratory, and instrumental tests** according to the diagnosis, beyond the clinical
   pathway.
7. **Choice of treating doctor / medical team — 461 €.**
8. **Treatment — 1 000 €:** medication, diet, hormonal treatment, physiotherapy, rehabilitation,
   immunotherapy.
9. **Surgical operations:** low / medium / high and very high complexity; **robotic surgery — 2 500 €**;
   laparoscopic and endoscopic; minimally invasive.
10. **Consumables and implants — sublimit 770 €** per insurance year.
11. **Expenses for an accompanying person — 105 €.**
12. **Medical transport — 52 €.**
13. **Nursing care — 100 €.**

## 5. Physiotherapy and rehabilitation — limit 231 € + 231 €

Covers physiotherapy for medical indications (outpatient or inpatient), performed **within 6 weeks** from
the date of prescription. Rehabilitation and sanatorium treatment — as prescribed, as a continuation of
recovery after intensive inpatient treatment.

**"Physiotherapy" section — 231 €:**
- Examination by a specialist in physical and rehabilitation medicine.
- Physiotherapy treatment: kinesitherapy, hydrotherapy, mud treatment, electrotherapy, laser therapy,
  magnetotherapy, ultrasound therapy, thermotherapy, ozone therapy.

**"Rehabilitation" section — 231 €:**
- Hospitalization in a licensed medical facility (rehabilitation for acute and chronic diseases,
  sanatorium treatment).
- Admission and accommodation — ward with 3+ beds or two-bed ward.
- Rounds with examination and prescriptions: duty doctor; head of department — once during the stay;
  licensed specialist — once during the stay.
- Procedures depending on diagnosis and treatment plan; clinical, laboratory, and clinical instrumental
  tests; treatment (medication; physiotherapy and rehabilitation).

**Timeframe:** rehabilitation treatment must be prescribed in the discharge epicrisis of completed
inpatient treatment and must start **no later than 3 months** after discharge.

## 6. Medicines and medical devices — limit 205 € per year per person

Under expense reimbursement terms.

- **"Medicines" section:** medicinal products, homeopathic products, vitamins and minerals during
  antibiotic therapy.
- **"Medical consumables" section:** dressing materials; contrast substances.
- **"Ophthalmology services" section:** for vision correction with a diopter **from 0,50**, the insured
  person is entitled to only **one of two** coverages — eyeglass lenses and frames **up to 103 €** OR
  contact lenses **up to 103 €**.
- **"Assistive devices" section:** assistive therapeutic devices (orthoses, splints, crutches, canes,
  catheters, colostomy bags, hernia bandages, etc.); limb prostheses and wheelchairs; hearing aids.

**Note:** only registered medicinal products from the official registers of the Medicines Agency (EAM),
prescribed by a doctor or specialist doctor, are reimbursed.

---

## 7. Telemedicine

1. **Online consultation with a doctor:** primary (for injury, illness, or symptoms) and follow-up — by a
   specialist doctor of all specialties or by a licensed specialist.
2. **Tests following a consultation:** clinical laboratory tests (hematology, biochemistry, microbiology,
   hormones, virology, serology; chemical urine analysis + sediment); clinical instrumental examinations
   (X-ray, ultrasound); morphological tests (cytology).
3. **Medical products following a consultation:** over-the-counter medicinal and homeopathic products
   from the EAM list (Republic of Bulgaria); dressing materials; assistive therapeutic devices.

---

## 8. Pregnancy and childbirth — limit 770 € per year per person

Under expense reimbursement terms, with a **waiting period (quarantine) of 9 months**.

- **Pregnancy monitoring** (normal and high-risk): medical examinations and ultrasound for monitoring;
  examinations prescribed by the gynecologist monitoring the pregnancy; prenatal fetal diagnostics;
  biochemical screening; fetal morphology; fetal echocardiography.
- **Childbirth:** a **fixed amount** is paid regardless of the actually incurred costs.

**Note:** highly specialized examinations (CT, NMR, MRI) — only after prior approval by the insurer.

---

## 9. Dentistry — limit 256 € per insurance year

Under expense reimbursement terms. All types of dental services are covered, except those explicitly
excluded by the General Terms.

- **Periodontology (preventive therapy):** preventive examination; advice on hygiene and treatment of
  tissues and mucosa; removal of tartar, plaque, and polishing; Air Flow polishing; treatment of aphthae;
  therapeutic curettage for inflammatory gum diseases.
- **Therapy:** examination and preparation of a treatment plan; caries prevention (sealing deep fissures
  with sealants); caries treatment; pulpitis treatment; periodontitis treatment.
- **Diagnostic examinations:** segmental X-ray; panoramic X-ray; computed tomography (3D scanner).
- **Orthopedic treatment:** repair of broken dentures and fixation of fallen crowns and bridges; tooth
  preparation and single metal-ceramic/zirconia crown (without dental technician services);
  metal-ceramic/zirconia bridge of 2 units (without dental technician services); fabrication and
  placement of a crown and bridge of 2 units.
- **Oral surgery:** tooth extraction per year (single-rooted and multi-rooted, deeply destroyed);
  intraoral incisions; apical osteotomy; reduction of jaw joint dislocation.
- **Anesthesia:** administration of anesthesia.
- **Other services:** emergency dental treatment (drilling, incisions, medicines, and anesthesia in an
  acute condition); physiotherapy — per insurance year; medicinal products for treatment of teeth and
  oral cavity.

## Required documents (by service type)

**General for all:** completed claim form + documents confirming the expenses.

**Outpatient treatment:**
- *Examinations:* outpatient sheet/referral (form 119/98) with diagnosis, history, objective condition;
  invoice with fiscal receipt and detailed list.
- *Tests:* referral with the list of required tests; results; imaging diagnostic reports (if available);
  invoice with receipt and list.
- For **breast ultrasound/mammography**, a referral from a surgeon, thoracic surgeon, or
  obstetrician-gynecologist is required.

**Inpatient treatment:** discharge epicrisis (signature of the treating doctor and head of department,
date, stamp); hospitalization referral; statement for choice of doctor/team (copy, if selected); results
and reports outside the clinical pathway (if prescribed); for expensive materials/implants outside NZOK —
stickers or product identification codes; invoice with receipt and all procedures and prices.

**Medicines:** outpatient sheet/epicrisis with diagnosis and prescribed products (dosage, period);
prescription / white prescription form (MOH sample) — quantity and regimen for each product, signature,
stamp, date; invoice with receipt and list; for contrast substances — referral for the examination and its
report.

**Corrective devices (glasses/lenses):** properly completed outpatient sheet; ophthalmologist prescription
with diopters; copy of stickers/packaging of lenses or contact lenses and/or copy of warranty card;
invoice with receipt and list.

**Physiotherapy:** referral (119/98) from the relevant specialist with a recommendation for physiotherapy;
outpatient sheet from a physical therapy and rehabilitation specialist with a list of procedures; card
with prescribed and performed procedures; invoice with receipt and list.

**Rehabilitation:** discharge epicrisis with a recommendation for rehabilitation; invoice with detailed
description, prices, and fiscal receipt (original); document confirming the rehabilitation performed.

**Psychotherapeutic assistance:** outpatient sheet/referral with doctor's signature and stamp (UIN);
invoice with fiscal receipt.

**Pregnancy and childbirth:** outpatient sheet or another document for examination; for laboratory tests /
ultrasound / imaging — referral (119/98), results, reports, invoice with list; childbirth — discharge
epicrisis, statement for choice of doctor/team (copy), invoice with receipt and all procedures and prices.

**Dentistry:** outpatient sheet with recorded dental status and description of services; invoice with
receipt and list; for segmental or panoramic X-ray — copy of the image; X-ray for pulpitis treatment,
tooth extraction, and other surgical interventions.

**Important:** if the medical facility cannot issue an invoice with a detailed list of all services during
the stay, a **payment document** with a clear list of all separate items must be issued.

## Timeframes

- Purchase of medicines — **up to 10 calendar days**.
- Purchase of eyeglass/contact lenses by prescription — **up to 60 calendar days** from the prescription
  date.
- Performing tests — **up to 30 calendar days** from the prescription date.
- Expense reimbursement — **up to 15 business days** from submitting correctly completed documents.
- Response to a CT/MRI approval request — **within 48 hours** (in case of delay, contact Mussala).

## How to submit a reimbursement claim

1. **Collect documents** — all supporting documents + completed claim form.
2. **Submit the claim** — UNIQA portal, or Mussala platform (**app.mussalains.com**), or
   **health@mussalains.com**.
3. **Review** — documents are checked for completeness; if needed, you will be contacted to add missing
   items.
4. **Payment** — up to **15 business days** from submitting correctly completed documents.

Reminder: original invoices **are not required for amounts up to 500 €** (except incomplete/unclear
copies). Highly specialized examinations (CT, NMR, MRI) — only after approval by the insurer; send the
outpatient sheet with prescription to health@mussalains.com.
